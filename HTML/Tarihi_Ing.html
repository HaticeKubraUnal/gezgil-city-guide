Side Ancient City


Side Ancient City is famous for its historical ruins dating back to the 7th century BC. nEnriched with important structures such as the Temple of Apollo, the Ancient Theater, the Agora and the Ancient Port, this area is a fascinating place to visit with both its historical and natural beauties. Side also draws attention with its lovely beaches on the Mediterranean coast and its friendly atmosphere.

Temple of Apollo


The Temple of Apollo in the Ancient City of Side was built in the 2nd century BC and was dedicated to the god Apollo. This temple, which draws attention with its Ionian style columns, is one of the important religious structures of the ancient world and fascinates its visitors with its enormous size and impressive architecture. The temple is located in a position dominating the Mediterranean view and is one of the symbols of the cultural heritage of the region.

Side Museum


Side Museum is located in the Roman bath building in Side Ancient City and exhibits many artifacts from ancient times. It has a rich collection of sculptures, sarcophagi, inscriptions and coins from the Late Bronze Age, Hellenistic, Roman and Byzantine periods. The museum is organized in sections of the Roman bath and thus provides visitors with information about the architecture of the period.

Oluk Bridge


Oluk Bridge is a structure from the Roman Empire period, built in the 2nd century BC and is located on the Köprüçay River. Designed with Ionic columns and arches, this bridge was built to prevent floods and is part of a waterway network that carries water to agriculture. Today, it is an important place to visit for tourists who come to the region for both its historical and natural beauties.

Selge Ancient City


It is a historical settlement located in the Pisidia region and was founded in the 4th century BC. It attracts attention with its Roman ruins and has a rich historical heritage with its structures such as ancient theater, city walls, and baths. Selge, which stands out with its high location and natural beauties, is an important place to visit for those who want to do both nature walks and historical discoveries.

Seleukeia - Lyrbe Ancient City


Seleukeia - Lyrbe Ancient City is an important ancient settlement founded in the 3rd century BC. The city is full of ruins from the Hellenistic, Roman and Byzantine periods and attracts attention with its structures such as ancient theater, temples and baths. The city is located on a high hill and offers a rich experience to visitors with both historical ruins and natural beauties.

Yörük Museum


Manavgat Private Yoruk Museum is a museum that aims to introduce Yoruk culture. The museum was established on an area of ​​approximately 4,000 m² and has a closed exhibition area of ​​464 m². In the museum where nearly 1,000 ethnographic works are exhibited, the traditional life of Yoruks is presented to visitors with various themes.

Kent Museum


Manavgat City Museum is a modern culture museum that was opened in 2018. The museum consists of 14 different sections that deal with themes such as Yoruk and Cretan culture, agriculture, crafts, food culture and natural life. It includes Yoruk tents, weaving looms, food presentations, agricultural tools, nature animations of the Taurus Mountains and visual archives. It offers a rich cultural experience to visitors with social areas such as projection areas, libraries and city cafes.
